## Followup Game: Beyond Algebraic Group Theory

A second lean4game is planned to cover group theory topics that don't fit the
current game's algebraic focus. This is a parking lot — nothing here is decided,
just recorded for future planning.

### Topics earmarked so far

- **HNN extensions** — fundamental construction in combinatorial/geometric group theory
- **Wreath products** — key construction for finite group classification and permutation groups
- **Group presentations** machinery beyond W40 — word problems, Tietze transformations, decidability
- **Finite group specifics** — classification of small groups, Burnside's theorem, transfer homomorphism

### Broad areas to draw from

- **Combinatorial group theory** — presentations, word problems, small cancellation, Bass-Serre theory
- **Geometric group theory** — Cayley graphs, quasi-isometry, growth, hyperbolic groups
- **Finite group theory** — Burnside, Hall subgroups, transfer, classification of groups of small order

### Prerequisites question (open)

The current game covers core algebraic group theory through Sylow, solvability,
and free groups. The followup game would assume all of that as prerequisite.

Additional prerequisites that may be needed depending on topic selection:
- **Geometry/topology lean4game?** — geometric group theory needs metric spaces, graphs, fundamental groups. Would a separate geometry game be a prerequisite, or would the followup game teach what it needs inline?
- **Combinatorics lean4game?** — counting arguments, graph theory, generating functions. Some finite group theory leans heavily on combinatorics. Same question: prerequisite game or inline?

These are not decisions to make now. They're recorded so the question isn't lost.
